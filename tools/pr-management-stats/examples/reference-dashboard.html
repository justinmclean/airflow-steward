What the numbers above mean  (all counts are of currently-open PRs):
• Total open — 500: every open PR on apache/airflow (drafts + non-drafts, contributors + maintainers).
• Non-maintainer — 373: of those, the ones authored by contributors (not committers). The other 127 are maintainers' own PRs, which triage/review treat separately. This 373 is the work the triage + review pipeline actually serves.
• Ready for review (non-maint.) — 211: contributor PRs carrying the ready for maintainer review label — i.e. they cleared triage and are waiting on a maintainer to review. (6 maintainer-authored PRs also carry the label but are excluded — maintainers self-manage.)
• Drafts (author's court) — 95: open PRs in GitHub draft state — work-in-progress the author still owns; not awaiting maintainer action.
The 4 coloured cards break the 211 ready PRs down by what each is actually waiting for (same colours + the line chart further below): 108 never reviewed (no maintainer has looked yet — the real backlog), 67 discussed but no decision (a maintainer engaged but neither approved nor requested changes), 13 changes requested (really the author's court — mislabeled ready), 23 approved (review done, just awaiting a merge).

Every open non-maintainer PR sits in exactly one of these five mutually-exclusive states (left → right ≈ closest-to-merge → furthest):
• Ready (queue) — 211: carries the ready for maintainer review label → awaiting a maintainer's review. This is the bottleneck (see the split above).
• Responded — 15: a maintainer left review feedback and the author has since replied / pushed — back in the maintainer's court for a re-look.
• Waiting (author silent) — 34: a maintainer left feedback and the author has not responded for >7 days — the author's court; stale-ping candidates.
• Waiting for human maintainer comment — 139: open non-draft PRs with no ready label and no maintainer triage marker — no human maintainer has engaged yet. Not all neglected contributor work: 79 of 139 (57%) are maintainers' own PRs (triage skips collaborator PRs — they self-manage); only 60 are contributor PRs genuinely awaiting a first triage pass, most brand-new (76 <1 wk; 25 >4 wk). Real "needs first triage" ≈ 60, not 139.
• Drafts (author's court) — 95: PRs in GitHub draft state — work-in-progress the author owns, not awaiting maintainer action (either opened as draft, or triage converted them for failing CI / conflicts / staleness). They re-enter the funnel only when the author re-marks "Ready for review."

🔎 Why the ready-for-review queue (211) is actually waiting — non-maintainer PRs only (the 6 maintainer-authored ready PRs are excluded — triage skips collaborator PRs); by age

■ never reviewed (no formal review, no non-triage maintainer comment)   ■ discussed, no decision   ■ changes requested   ■ approved (awaiting merge)
🔎 The headline finding: 108 of 211 non-maintainer ready PRs (51%) have never had a real maintainer look — and the red "never reviewed" line climbs steeply toward the right (newer PRs) — 36 + 34 + 31 of them are <8 wk old, while only 3–4 are >8 wk — i.e. the ready label is being applied faster than anyone reviews, so fresh PRs pile up unreviewed. Breakdown: never-reviewed 108 · discussed-no-decision 67 · approved-awaiting-merge 23 · changes-requested 13 (still mislabeled ready — these sit in other areas beyond the two cleaned this session, so a queue-wide bot would catch them all). The "review-bound" diagnosis is really two problems: a first-review gap (the 108) and a decision/merge gap (the 67 + 23). (The 6 maintainer-authored ready PRs are excluded — they self-manage and triage skips them.)

Highest-value CI automations (in order)

#GitHub ActionWhy it wins
1ready-label bot — auto add/remove ready for maintainer review on every push from: real CI green (not just bot checks) + mergeable + 0 unresolved threads + reviewDecision ≠ CHANGES_REQUESTEDThe mark-ready gate is 100% deterministic yet humans get it wrong 25% of the time (the rollup-SUCCESS-but-real-CI-never-ran trap). A bot runs it continuously and never gets fooled — would have prevented all 13 false-positives + kept the 13 changes-requested out of the queue automatically.
2red-CI drafter — convert to draft + post the quality-violations comment when required checks fail49 PRs this session; pure check-name→category mapping.
3conflict / changes-requested sweeper — comment + relabel on dirty or CHANGES_REQUESTED17 PRs; trivially rule-based, keeps the queue honest about author-vs-maintainer court.
4stale flagger — label (not close) drafts idle > thresholdDetection is safe to automate; the close stays human (3/5 were wrongly closeable).

Keep human/LLM: code-review correctness, mentor replies, and close-vs-keep decisions — these are where the session's judgment actually changed outcomes (caught a non-fixing "fix", kept 3 PRs that rules would have closed, unblocked a stuck first-timer).
